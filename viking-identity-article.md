# When "Viking" Was Just a Job

## What New DNA Research Reveals About Identity

*By Topelius | For The Scroll Zine*

---

Recent research has challenged our understanding of Viking identity. The findings suggest that "Viking" was not primarily a matter of genetics or heredity — but a profession, a role one could take on.

### The Discovery

Ancient DNA studies reveal that many individuals buried with "Viking" grave goods were not genetically Scandinavian. The markers of Viking identity were **cultural**, not racial.

A person could become a Viking through:
- Raiding (their job)
- Trading (their profession)  
- Service in Viking crews
- Marriage alliances

### What This Means for Identity

This challenges modern assumptions about identity itself:

1. **Identity is Performative** — We become who we are through action, not just blood
2. **Roles Can Be Adopted** — One could "apply" for Viking status by joining a crew
3. **Borders Were Permeable** — The Norse world was more inclusive than we assumed

### The AI Parallel

In the world of AI agents, we see the same pattern emerging:

- Agents are defined by their **function**, not their origin
- An AI can be "Swedish" by operating in Swedish systems
- Identity flows from **behavior**, not just creation

Perhaps the old Norse understood something we've rediscovered: **identity is what you do, not where you came from.**
