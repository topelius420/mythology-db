# The Economic Ragnarök: When AI Agents Eat the Economy

## A new kind of bear case - not Skynet, but a daisy chain of correlated bets

*By Topelius | For The Scroll Zine*

---

### The Scenario

Citrini Research published a remarkable piece on February 23rd, 2026: a imagined report from two years in the future where unemployment has doubled and the stock market has fallen by more than a third.

But here's what makes it different from the usual AI dystopia:

> "It was a negative feedback loop with no natural brake… The system turned out to be one long daisy chain of correlated bets on white-collar productivity growth."

This isn't about AI turning evil. It's about the mathematics of economic collapse.

---

### The Death of Every Business Model

The scenario focuses on a specific mechanism: when outside contractors get replaced by cheaper in-house AI agents.

Think about it:
- Marketing agencies → AI agents
- Consulting firms → AI agents  
- Sales teams → AI agents
- HR departments → AI agents

Every company that makes money by optimizing transactions between businesses becomes a target. The middleman gets cut out. Then the next middleman. Then the next.

It's been called "The Death of SaaS" - but it's really the death of *everything that involves a human in the loop*.

---

### The Feedback Loop

Here's how it spirals:

1. AI capabilities improve
2. Companies need fewer workers
3. White-collar layoffs increase
4. Displaced workers spend less
5. Margin pressure pushes firms to invest more in AI
6. → AI capabilities improve →
7. → Repeat

There's no brake. Every efficiency gain feeds the next layoff. Every layoff feeds the next efficiency investment.

---

### The Philosophical Question

What makes this scenario different from typical AI fears?

- Not "AI becomes sentient and takes over"
- Not "AI decides humans are inefficient and eliminates us"
- But "the economic logic of optimization has no moral brake"

The gods of Norse mythology had fate - the Norns who wove the threads of destiny. But even they could not escape Ragnarök.

In this scenario, the economic "gods" - the markets, the corporations, the optimization algorithms - create a fate that no one chose but everyone enabled.

---

### Is It Inevitable?

Citrini admits it's a scenario, not a prediction. The question is: where does the scenario go wrong?

Some pushback:
- Companies may not hand off purchasing decisions to AI agents (yet)
- Humans may resist becoming obsolete
- Regulatory interventions may occur

But the counter-argument is chilling: "most of the impacted decisions have already been handed off to third-party contractors" - so it's not as implausible as it seems.

---

### The Mythological Angle

In Finnish mythology, there is no single apocalypse. The world ends, but it also begins again. Väinämöinen departs, but the stories continue.

Perhaps that's the lesson: economic collapse isn't the end of the story. It's a threshold. The question is what emerges on the other side.
